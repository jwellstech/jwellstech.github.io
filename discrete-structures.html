Term Definition
proposition a statement that is either true or false
tautology all valuations satisfy it
contradiction no valuation satisfies it
equivalent two formulas are equivalent if they are satisfied by the same valuations
logical consequence B is a logical consequence of A if every valuation that satisfies A also satisfies B
contrapositive ¬q ⊃ ¬p is contrapositive of p ⊃ q
converse p ⊃ q is converse of q ⊃ p
inverse (p ⊃ q) is inverse of (¬p ⊃ ¬q)

Property Example
deMorgan laws (p ∧ q) ≡ ¬(¬p ∨ ¬q)
(p ∨ q) ≡ ¬(¬p ∧ ¬q)
Commutativity (p ∨ q) ≡ (q ∨ p)
(p ∧ q) ≡ (q ∧ p)
Associativity (p ∨ (q ∨ r)) ≡ ((p ∨ q) ∨ r)
(p ∧ (q ∧ r)) ≡ (p ∧ (q ∧ r))
Distributivity (p ∧ (q ∨ r)) ≡ ((p ∧ q) ∨ (p ∧ r))
(p ∨ (q ∧ r)) ≡ ((p ∨ q) ∧ (p ∨ r))
Idempotence (p ∧ p) ≡ p
(p ∨ p) ≡ p
Absorption (p ∧ (p ∨ q)) ≡ (p ∨ (p ∧ q)) ≡ p
